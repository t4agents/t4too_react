 Overview
 Extraction quality is crucial for our system's performance, impacting document understanding, feature retrieval, and response quality. We'll set goals to improve extraction quality, focusing on key metrics. Due to challenges in automating evaluation, human assessment will play a significant role in measuring progress and providing insights for improvement.

For this task, the team will evaluate the quality of the URL content parser's extraction, focusing on two key aspects:

1. Completeness: Is any crucial content missing from the extracted body text?
2. Precision: How much unnecessary or irrelevant information is retained in the extracted body text?

This evaluation will be based on a given URL and user prompt pair.

## Content Extraction Precision Questions

For this section, you need to ask yourself: "How much unnecessary or irrelevant information is retained in the extracted body text?"

Below are definitions of the elements commonly found on a website:

- Header: This is the top section of a webpage that usually appears on every page of a website. This can be the website logo or the title of the page.
- Footer: The bottom section of a webpage. Common content here is copyright information, terms of service, and many more.
- Navigation Menu: A set of links/texts that guide users to different sections or pages of the website. You can see it as horizontal menus, vertical side menus, or drop-down menus.
- Side bar: A vertical column that shows additional or related information on the website. This is usually found on the left or right side of the main content.
- Advertisement or Promotional Materials: These are content meant to promote products, services, or external brands.

## Tables

When reviewing tables, you may encounter two types:

- Properly formatted tables: These have clear rows and columns, similar to standard table layouts.
- Unformatted or cell-by-cell/by-line tables: These tables appear as a series of individual lines or cells, without proper table structure.
